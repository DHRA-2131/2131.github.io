## Step 1: Check The Port Connectivity

Plug in a working motor (or other smart port device) and wire that have good connectivity into the port. The device should show up in the device menu. If the device doesn't show up move on to Step 2. If the device shows up and the Motor is unresponsive (like below), then the port is blown. Move on to Step 3 if this is the case.

// Video of Motor Not Spinning

## Step 2: Lifting The Pins in The Port

If the pins in the motor look like the image below, then the port could have bad contact with the wire. This will make it so the device cannot connect to the brain.

// Photo of Smashed Port

To remedy the port, grab the pick tool from narnia (image below) or tweasers. 

// Photo of Lock pick

Working left to right, lift each wire in the port to the point above the small ridges in the port. 

// Image of lifted Pins

This should repair connectivity. Go back to Step 1 and verify that the device is working properly.

## Step 3: Repairing Electro-Staticly Damaged Ports

> [!IMPORTANT]
> IMPORTANT: Ensure that the port is fully dead. Only follow these steps once the brain has little to no ports left. _THIS WILL MAKE YOUR BRAIN NO LONGER COMPETITION LEGAL!_

Remove the screws on the back of the brain. Remove the back plating as shown below. For each damaged chip follow the following.

// Picture of brain without screws

// Picture of brain without backing

Take the heat gun and remove the chip. You will want tweasers for this. Be careful as to not pull up the contacts.

// Picture of tweasers

// Picture of Heatgun

// Picture of removing chip with heatgun


Take soldering-wick and remove the excess solder from the contacts. Be careful to not pull up the contacts. After the solder has been pulled up clean the contacts with isopropal alcohol. 

// Picture of soldering iron 

// Picture of cleaning port

// Picture of clean Port

Apply soldering paste to the contacts. _Less is more!_ Apply flux to the contacts on the chip. You might want latex gloves as soldering flux is sticky and annoying.

// Picture of Apply soldering paste

Solder on the chip. Be careful and check the orientation of the chip. The chip should orient the same as all the others. You will want ventalation for this step (a fan is recommended).

// Picture of soldering on the chip

Once all chips are re-attached, mark the brain as opened (Spray paint the back or something -- this has not been decided yet). Once the paint is dry, re-attach the baseplate.
